Hello Heroes!

In this first issue of the Fyne Heroes newsletter we are excited to highlight two great projects we have seen being constructed with the toolkit. We also keep you updated on the latest changes with the toolkit and introduce Geoffrey, a Fyne automated build and deploy solution.

Steam Deck Utilities

This exciting project by CryoByte33 was recently moved to a Fyne UI to make it easier for gamers to manage performance of the Valve Steam Deck product.

You can see their excellent video showing how it easy it makes these complex issues.
   

World Wide Technologies - Helios

We love to see Fyne being used in large companies of course, and the Helios app is a great example of that!

WWT, one of the US largest private companies, experienced a boost in productivity for their industrial use-case of hardware testing. They shared that "Fyne is just so much simpler and to the point" (compared to .NET alternative).

Fyne release v2.3.2

The latest release of Fyne includes a long list of fixes. Headlining these changes is completed support for Apple M2 devices, resolving all known issues when running on Apple Silicon.

Building with Geoffrey

If your team could benefit from automated build and deployment, with download hosting and self-update - you should meet Geoffrey.

The one-click solution to build all your platforms from a single button or web-hook.
